I am currently studying in a professional doctorate program at the University of Edinburgh. I aspire to reduce emotional distress, enhance psychological well-being, and promote a positive perception of mental health. I strive to uplift lives because I truly believe that every individual deserves a better and healthier life. My life goal is to help people gain self-reliance, dignity and hope through therapy.My approach is psychodynamic and person-centred. In fact, I don’t like to categorise. So more accurately speaking, my approach is relational. It is two people sitting together trying to discover who we really are, what is perpepuating our dysfunctional thoughts or behaviours, and what is hindering us from self-actualisation and feeling fulfilled. Psychotherapy, in my opinion, is not about fixing something. It’s all about developing our self-awareness, the capacity to reflect on ourselves and to make better decisions in life, without repeating our unhealthy patterns.We together try to make sense of something painful and unbearable, create meanings out of it, and thus experience psychic transformation. Hmmm.....it might sound vague. To give you an analogy, imagine both of us trying to map a dark foggy city together with a compass and find the way out. Though it might be scary, it is a transformative journey. In our work, this is a safe space for us to let this exquisite alchemical reaction happen. It is simple......but deep…...very deep......This is a poem I wrote at the beginning of my training.
